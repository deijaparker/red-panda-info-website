About Red Pandas

The Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens) is a small, tree-dwelling mammal native to the forests of the Himalayas and southwestern China. Known for its striking reddish-brown fur and bushy tail, the red panda is an agile climber and primarily nocturnal.

Species

  • Himalayan Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens fulgens)
  • Chinese Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens styani)
  • Eastern Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens refulgens)
  • Western Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens nigrescens)

Features

Red pandas have a false thumb, an extended wrist bone, which helps them grasp bamboo. They are also known for their ability to descend trees head-first.

Habitat

Red pandas inhabit temperate forests with dense bamboo growth at high altitudes.

Countries with Large Red Panda Populations

  1. China
  2. Nepal
  3. Bhutan

Countries with Small Red Panda Populations

Countries with smaller red panda populations include Myanmar, India and Bangladesh.
